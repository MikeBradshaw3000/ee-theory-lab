## The fork

Should the Layer 3 design-menu contract scope **one rule, two rules, or all three**?

**One - likely B.** B is the rule whose expected per-observable consequence (shifting structures: meanI-high / persistence-low) sits in the quadrant the held inputs explicitly mark as "ontologically unresolved." Running B first puts data on the most interesting quadrant fastest. Trade-off: forecloses on a comparison; the menu's per-observable-divergence point produces evidence from one rule family only.

**All three.** The menu's purpose is per-observable divergence across rule families. A produces both-agree (low/low at low Lambda, high/high at high Lambda; no Lambda-varying divergence). B produces meanI-high / persistence-low. C produces tunable divergence. Running all three gives the Lambda-varying sequence across rule families, and the rule-family x Lambda interaction is itself plausibly informative. Trade-off: bigger scope, longer feedback loop, more uncommitted joints if any one rule turns out to require redesign.

**Two - A plus one of B / C.** A functions as a substrate-side sanity baseline of the same type C3-CTL-001 provided observable-side: a non-discriminating control against which the discriminating rule's signature is read. Pairing A with B or C gives the discrimination structure the held inputs anticipated. Trade-off: defers a B-vs-C comparison to a second wave.
